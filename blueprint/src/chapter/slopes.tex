\chapter{Slopes and eigenvalues}

In this section we combine the work on compact operators and Newton polygons.
Recall:
\begin{itemize}
  \item slopes of the Newton polygon gives information on the valuation of roots of the
    power series; %reference
  \item compact operators can be associated with a Fredholm determinant, that is a characteristic
    power series.
\end{itemize}

Therefore, since roots of the characteristic power series % is this still obvious with this definition
correspond to eigenvalues of the operator, we can construct the Newton polygon of the characteristic
power series to get information on the valuations of the eigenvalues.

This can be summed up in the following statement.

\begin{lemma}
  Let $u$ be a compact operator on a Banach space. Then the slopes of the Newton polygon of $u$,
  that is the slopes of the characteristic power series of $u$, are precisely the $p$-adic valuations
  of the eigenvalues of $u$.
\end{lemma}
